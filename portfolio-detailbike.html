Bike Buyer Analysis Project

             
                Data Analysis                 Buyer Segmentation                 View on GitHub              
           
                       
             

Project Overview & Goals

             

This project focused on analyzing bike buyer data to identify **key demographic and behavioral segments most likely to purchase a bicycle. The goal was to provide actionable insights for optimizing marketing and sales strategies.

                           

Core Business Questions:

             
                   
  • How does a potential buyer's age influence their decision to purchase a bike?
    •                
  • Is there a correlation between income and the likelihood of purchasing a bike?
    •                
  • Do different commute distances show varying rates of bike purchases?
    •                
  • How do marital status and gender relate to the purchasing decision?
  • 1,000 Unique Records
  • 13 Features Analyzed
  • 26 Duplicates Removed
    •              
           
                       
             

Data Preparation Highlights

             
                   
  • Data Standardization: Confusing categorical codes were replaced for clarity (e.g., Marital Status: 'M' to 'Married', 'S' to 'Single'; Gender: 'M' to 'Male', 'F' to 'Female').
    •                
  • Data Quality: Identified and removed 26 duplicate entries, resulting in 1,000 unique records.
    •                
  • Feature Engineering: The raw `Age` column was grouped into the `Age Bracket` categories (ADOLESCENCE, MIDDLE AGE, OLD) to simplify visualization and analysis of trends.
    •                
  • Type Conversion: The `Income` column was explicitly converted to a currency format to enable accurate average calculations.
    •              
           
                       
             

Key Findings & Analysis

             

Analysis was performed using Pivot Tables to segment the data by age bracket, commute distance, and average income.

                           
               
                                   
                   

1. Purchase by Age Bracket

                   

The Middle Age group showed the highest purchase volume (16 out of 25 records), confirming they are the primary target demographic.

                 
               
             
             
               
                                   
                   

2. Average Income vs. Purchase

                   

Bike purchasers had a significantly higher average income ($72,105) compared to non-purchasers ($55,714), highlighting income as a strong predictor of purchase likelihood.

                 
               
             
           
                       
             

Actionable Insights

                           

Optimal Target Demographic:

             
                   
  • The most profitable segment is the Middle Age bracket, specifically targeting customers with an average income of approximately $72,105.
    •              
             

Marketing Strategy Focus:

             
                   
  • Commute Indicator: The largest purchasing groups fall into the 1-2 miles and 5-10 miles commute distance, suggesting the bicycle is primarily a commuting tool.
    •                
  • Campaign Theme: Marketing should emphasize the convenience, health benefits, and cost-saving aspects of cycling for mid-range commutes (1 to 10 miles).
    •
