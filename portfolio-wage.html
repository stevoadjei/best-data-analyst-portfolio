A Comprehensive Analysis of Minimum Wage Policy Using the Modern Wage Dynamics Model

             
                Policy Analysis                 Econometric Modeling                 View Paper (PDF)              
           
                       
             

Project Abstract & Objectives

             

This study employs the **Modern Wage Dynamics Model** to conduct a comprehensive econometric analysis of minimum wage policy. The primary focus is to move beyond traditional equilibrium models and empirically evaluate the true impact of mandated minimum wages on **employment levels**, **wage distribution**, and **poverty reduction** within a dynamic labor market framework.

             

The project utilized extensive panel data, applying advanced time-series and panel regression techniques to isolate causal effects. Key objectives included estimating the **employment elasticity of minimum wage changes** and identifying heterogeneous impacts across different demographic groups and regional markets.

Econometric Analysis & Key Findings

                           

The findings from the dynamic model suggest a complex, often non-linear, relationship between minimum wage hikes and labor market outcomes. Key results include the estimated **employment elasticity** and visualizations of wage compression effects.

Policy Conclusion

             

The analysis provides critical evidence challenging simplified views on minimum wage effects. The dynamic model shows that while wage floors successfully raise income for low-wage earners, policymakers must be mindful of the **heterogeneous employment response** and focus policy adjustments on minimizing negative effects for vulnerable groups, particularly younger workers and those in elastic markets.
